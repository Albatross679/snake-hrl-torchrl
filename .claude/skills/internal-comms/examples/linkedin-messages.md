## Instructions

You are being asked to write a LinkedIn message in Qifan's personal style. LinkedIn messages differ from professional emails—they're more conversational, concise, and personal while remaining respectful and professional.

## Style Profile

### Tone
- **Warm and approachable** - like talking to a colleague, not writing a formal letter
- **Concise** - LinkedIn messages are read on mobile; respect their time
- **Personal** - reference shared connections, interests, or context when relevant
- **Confident but humble** - no over-apologizing, but not pushy either

### Key Differences from Email
| Email | LinkedIn Message |
|-------|------------------|
| "Dear Professor Smith," | "Hi Sarah," or "Hey John," |
| Multiple paragraphs | 2-4 short sentences max per message |
| Formal closings | No closing needed, or just "Thanks!" |
| Detailed context | Brief, punchy context |
| "Would you be able to..." | "Would love to..." or "Happy to..." |

### Structure
1. **Opening:** Casual greeting + their first name
2. **Hook:** One sentence establishing connection or context
3. **Purpose:** Direct but friendly request (1-2 sentences)
4. **Close:** Optional brief sign-off or just end naturally

### Language Patterns

**Openings:**
- "Hi [Name],"
- "Hey [Name],"
- "Hi [Name] - "

**Establishing connection:**
- "Saw your post about [topic] and..."
- "We met at [event] - "
- "I noticed we're both connected to [person]..."
- "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out..."
- "Really enjoyed your talk on [topic]."

**Making requests:**
- "Would love to connect and hear more about..."
- "Would love to chat sometime if you're open to it."
- "Happy to share more about [topic] if helpful."
- "Let me know if you'd be up for a quick call."
- "No pressure either way!"

**Closings (all optional):**
- "Thanks!"
- "Cheers,"
- "Looking forward to connecting!"
- (Or just end naturally—no closing needed)

## Workflow

1. **Identify the relationship** - Cold outreach? Warm intro? Existing connection?
2. **Find the hook** - What makes this message relevant to them specifically?
3. **Keep it short** - If it's more than 5-6 lines, trim it down
4. **Read it aloud** - Does it sound like a real person talking?

## Message Types & Templates

### Cold Outreach (Networking)
```
Hi [Name],

[One sentence about why you're reaching out - be specific about what caught your attention]. Would love to connect and learn more about [their work/experience].

[Optional: Brief context about yourself if relevant]

Thanks!
```

### Warm Introduction (Mutual Connection)
```
Hi [Name],

[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out - [brief context about the referral]. Would love to connect if you're open to it.

[One sentence about what you'd like to discuss]
```

### Follow-up After Meeting
```
Hey [Name],

Great meeting you at [event]! Really enjoyed our conversation about [topic].

Would love to stay in touch - let me know if you'd ever want to grab coffee and continue the conversation.
```

### Asking for Advice/Informational
```
Hi [Name],

I've been following your work on [topic] and really admire [specific thing]. I'm currently [brief context about your situation].

Would you be open to a quick chat? Happy to work around your schedule. No pressure either way!
```

### Reconnecting with Old Contact
```
Hey [Name],

Hope you're doing well! It's been a while since [context of how you know them].

Saw you're now at [company/role] - congrats! Would love to catch up sometime if you're free.
```

## What to Avoid

- **Generic messages** - "I'd like to add you to my professional network"
- **Wall of text** - Keep it scannable
- **Overly formal language** - "I am writing to inquire..." sounds robotic
- **Immediate asks** - Don't ask for jobs/favors in the first message
- **Copy-paste vibes** - Personalize each message
- **Excessive exclamation points** - One or two max
- **Self-deprecation** - "Sorry to bother you..." just get to the point

## Examples

**Good:**
> Hi Sarah, saw your post about the challenges of scaling ML teams - really resonated with what we're dealing with at my company. Would love to connect and hear more about how you approached it at Stripe. Thanks!

**Too formal:**
> Dear Ms. Johnson, I hope this message finds you well. I am reaching out because I noticed your expertise in machine learning operations. I would be grateful if you could spare some time for a conversation. Best regards, Qifan

**Too generic:**
> Hi, I'd like to connect with you on LinkedIn. I think we could benefit from being in each other's networks.

**Too long:**
> Hi Sarah, my name is Qifan and I'm a software engineer at a startup in SF. I've been working on ML infrastructure for about 3 years now, mostly focused on model deployment and monitoring. I saw your post about scaling ML teams and it really resonated with me because we're going through similar challenges. Specifically, we're struggling with... [continues for 3 more paragraphs]
