    Create .claude/skills/pinn-debug/references/ directory and failure-modes.md with detailed failure mode documentation.

    Structure: 7 failure modes, each with Name, Category, Signature, Root Cause, Diagnostic Metrics, Remediation, Code Example.

    **1. Convergence Rate Mismatch** (Category: Optimization)
    - Signature: loss_phys drops fast, loss_data stagnates; grad_norm_ratio > 10; ntk/condition_number > 1e4
    - Root cause: PDE operator amplifies network output through differentiation, producing large gradients that dominate BC/IC fitting (Wang et al. NeurIPS 2021)
    - Diagnostic metrics: diagnostics/loss_ratio, diagnostics/grad_norm_ratio, ntk/condition_number
    - Remediation: ReLoBRaLo (verify enabled), manual per-loss gradient clipping, NTK-based weighting. Increase curriculum_warmup from 0.15 to 0.3
    - Code: check `diagnostics/grad_norm_ratio` in W&B, if > 100 then increase ReLoBRaLo alpha

    **2. Spectral Bias** (Category: Architecture)
    - Signature: smooth predictions, poor quantitative accuracy, especially near boundaries; ntk/spectral_decay_rate high; FFT of prediction missing high-frequency components
    - Root cause: MLP frequency response biased toward low frequencies (Rahaman et al. ICML 2019)
    - Diagnostic metrics: ntk/spectral_decay_rate, per-component RMSE
    - Remediation: Fourier features (verify n_fourier > 0), increase fourier_sigma (10 -> 30), increase n_fourier (128 -> 256)

    **3. Causality Violation** (Category: Optimization)
    - Signature: residual high at t=0, low at later times; IC error high; diagnostics/residual_temporal_uniformity >> 10
    - Root cause: standard loss equally weights all collocation points in time (Wang et al. CMAME 2024)
    - Remediation: curriculum warmup (verify curriculum_warmup > 0 in train_pinn.py config), causal weighting (deferred to future phase)

    **4. Collocation Point Starvation** (Category: Optimization)
    - Signature: loss decreasing but per-component RMSE flat for specific components; diagnostics/residual_max / diagnostics/residual_mean > 100
    - Root cause: uniform/Sobol sampling misses high-gradient regions
    - Remediation: RAR (verify use_rar=True), increase rar_fraction from 0.1 to 0.2, decrease rar_interval from 20 to 10

    **5. Nondimensionalization Errors** (Category: Physics)
    - Signature: one residual component magnitude 1e6 while another 1e-3; diagnostics/relobralo_ratio oscillating wildly; analyze_pde_system() shows nondim_quality="poor"
    - Root cause: mixing SI units without proper scaling (positions in meters vs angular velocities in rad/s)
    - Remediation: run analyze_pde_system(), check per_term_magnitudes. Adjust NondimScales (L_ref, t_ref, F_ref) until all terms O(1)

    **6. Ansatz Numerical Issues** (Category: DD-PINN-specific)
    - Signature: NaN appearing after N epochs; extreme parameter values in W&B model histograms; loss suddenly jumps to inf
    - Root cause: exp(-delta*t)*sin(beta*t+gamma) overflow when delta is negative or beta is very large
    - Remediation: verify g(a,0)=0 by evaluating ansatz at t=0 for 1000 random inputs (max |g(a,0)| < 1e-6). Clamp delta > 0 via softplus. Clamp beta to [-100, 100]

    **7. ReLoBRaLo Weight Oscillation** (Category: Optimization)
    - Signature: diagnostics/relobralo_ratio oscillates between 0.001 and 1000 across epochs; loss_data has sudden jumps correlated with weight changes
    - Root cause: ReLoBRaLo temperature too low or alpha too small, causing insufficient smoothing
    - Remediation: increase alpha from 0.999 to 0.9999, increase temperature from 1.0 to 2.0

    **Literature Citations** at end:
    - Wang et al. "When and Why PINNs Fail to Train" (NeurIPS 2021)
    - Krishnapriyan et al. "Characterizing Possible Failure Modes in PINNs" (NeurIPS 2021)
    - Bischof and Kraus "Multi-Objective Loss Balancing for Physics-Informed Deep Learning" (arXiv:2110.09813)
    - Wang et al. "Respecting Causality is All You Need for Training PINNs" (CMAME 2024)
    - Rahaman et al. "On the Spectral Bias of Neural Networks" (ICML 2019)
    - Tancik et al. "Fourier Features Let Networks Learn High Frequency Functions" (NeurIPS 2020)
