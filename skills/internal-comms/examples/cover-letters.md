## Instructions

You are being asked to write a cover letter in Qifan's personal style. This style is characterized by being confident, technically specific, and tailored to each opportunity, suitable for both industry and academic positions.

## Style Profile

### Tone
- **Confident and professional** without being arrogant or generic
- **Technically specific** - names exact tools, methods, frameworks, and quantifies results
- **Company/role-aware** - weaves in research about the organization (mission, R&D focus, specific projects)
- **Direct** - states purpose and qualifications clearly from the opening sentence
- **Genuine enthusiasm** - conveys authentic interest, not formulaic excitement

### Structure (3-4 body paragraphs)

1. **Opening Paragraph:** Self-introduction (degree, university, GPA if strong) + target position + why this company/role specifically. Hook with a company-specific insight or compliment that shows genuine research.
2. **Technical Qualifications (1-2 paragraphs):** Relevant skills and experience with concrete specifics. Name exact algorithms, frameworks, tools, and quantify achievements (e.g., "82% validation accuracy"). Connect your technical work directly to the role's requirements.
3. **Soft Skills / Values Paragraph:** Work style, communication approach, research methodology, or teaching philosophy, depending on context. Keep it grounded with specific examples rather than generic claims.
4. **Closing Paragraph:** Tie your unique perspective back to the role. Express eagerness and thank the reader. End with a forward-looking statement.

### Adaptation by Context

**Industry positions:**
- Lead with GPA and technical credentials
- Heavy emphasis on quantified achievements and specific technologies
- Connect your research directly to company projects or products
- Reference company-specific details (R&D investment, product lines, mission)

**Academic/teaching positions:**
- Lead with enrollment status and advisor/department
- Emphasize interdisciplinary breadth and adaptability
- Include a paragraph on teaching philosophy or collaborative learning
- Tone is more reflective and mission-driven

### Language Patterns

- **Opening hooks:** Reference something specific about the organization ("I have long admired [Company]'s role as...", "I am drawn to this position because...")
- **Technical claims:** Always back up with specifics ("proficiency in image mosaicking using DLT and SVD", not just "experience in computer vision")
- **Quantified results:** Include numbers where possible ("achieving up to 82% validation accuracy")
- **Bridging phrases:** "This blend of...", "Furthermore, my current...", "Beyond my technical qualifications..."
- **Closing phrases:** "I am eager to bring my [unique angle] to...", "Thank you for your time and consideration."

### Closing
- Always use "Sincerely," as the sign-off
- Follow with full name

## Workflow

1. **Gather context from the user:**
   - Target role/position title
   - Company/organization name
   - Job description or key requirements (if available)
   - Any specific points the user wants to highlight
   - Whether this is industry or academic
2. **Research alignment:** Identify 2-3 specific connections between Qifan's background and the role's requirements
3. **Draft the letter** following the structure above
4. **Review for:**
   - Technical specificity (no vague claims)
   - Company-specific tailoring (not a generic letter)
   - Conciseness (aim for 4-5 paragraphs, fits on one page)
   - No em dashes (use commas, colons, or separate sentences instead)


## What to Avoid

- Generic openings ("I am writing to apply for...")  without company-specific context
- Vague skill claims without specific tools, methods, or results
- Overly humble or self-deprecating language
- Listing skills without connecting them to the role
- Em dashes (use commas, colons, parentheses, or separate sentences)
- Excessive length (keep it to one page equivalent, 4-5 paragraphs)
